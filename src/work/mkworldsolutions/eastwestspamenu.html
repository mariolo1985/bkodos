Overview

I was approached by the owner of EastWest Spa to redesign their spa menu for clients to easily view. The requirements were:

  • Keep it to a page.
  • Have all required services and prices listed.

With these requirements in mind, I started with the research.

Research

I started this process a little different than normal thanks to having EastWestâ€™s old Spa Menu. I used their old menu as a starting point for the new menu. I did this by taking note of all the little notes the owner had for the new spa such as different prices or different services. Having the content was a really nice bonus in this case due to knowing the limitations and what categories were needed along with how many options within them.

After figuring out the required information, I researched current layouts for spa menus.

Sketching & Wireframes

With the research, I gathered I thought of many wireframe ideas for the layout for the menu. I also started to think of color schemes and what would go well together to help contrast the background with the text.

The main questions that I worked with in this phase were to either have the menu portrait or landscape. With this in mind, I created several ideas for both to test out.

Throughout the process, I met up with the owner for EastWest Spa to go over potential ideas for layouts. With her help, I decided landscape was a better choice to go due to readability and the ability to house potentially more information.

Final UI & Hand Off

For the final UI I took what critiques the client had for the menu and implemented them in and took her decision for the final look with these in place I was able to create the final product for EastWest Spa.

Conclusion

After this project, I learned how to implement what ideas the client had for the menu and create the visual product.

I also learned the difficulties of dealing with the client first hand and the continuous changes that the client had. I learned it is better to state beforehand how many revisions to do before the project ends so that you are not continually revising the final product multiple times.
