Overview
A notebook is a tool found in the OX Sports application. This tool can be used on the computer, tablet, and phone. The function of the Notebooks in OX Sports is to help coaches and players create notes whether they are personal or shared.
Research

Research for notebook came from seeing what functionality was currently available along with what we could possibly do to create a notebook that would be helpful for a coach and their team.

The notebook they had currently didnâ€™t have much in terms of functionality and worked a lot like Mac Notebook.

Majority of text processors have a way to manipulate the look of the text, how to align them and adding pictures were all standards with them so adding these feature seemed like an obvious choice, also with the work on playbooks we decided adding drawing would be an added benefit.

Sketching & Wireframes

I did some design research by looking at several resources such as the Mac Notebook, Google Docs, and Microsoft Word. I knew I wanted something simple like the Notebook on Mac but also combining the interactively of Google docs also looked appealing. This helped me with my sketching phase trying to piece together all the functionality also by keeping in with the confines of our style and pattern library that was being worked on.

Most of the collaborative notes came from Google Docs on how they add people and how the users have a random color depending on the user. The look is similar to Mac Notebook but also keeping with the OX Sports look and functionality of the side nav and the Info Panel.

This information helped me design our look and feel of notebook for OX Sports.

After completing the wireframes I put them into InVision to share to stakeholders and the group after iterating on feedback I started working on the final UI.

Final UI & Hand Off

Updating notebook with the final UI was simple at this point of the process, the notebook was one of the last modules we made for OX Sports because of this the look and patterns were already finalized and updating them with the final UI was quick and simple.

The main challenge with the notebook was showing off the different things they could do functionality wise to share with the devs so that they had a good idea of how all the tools work.

After updating the InVision prototype with the final UI and getting approval and any feedback from stakeholders and group members I was then in the Annotation phase.

Annotating any functionality with the best of my ability for the dev team to understand what each feature does and when. After that, I uploaded all the deliverables and link to inVision to Jira moved the Jira task to 'Ready for Dev'.

Any feedback or questions from the dev team usually came from a Word document. If there was ever any more understanding needed we would have a Skype call with any questions.

Conclusion

The notebook was also one of my first projects on OX Sports. It was a fun challenge to be given a feature that was already designed and redesign it to the new UI and upgrade the feature set to be more helpful to the users and their problems.

The notebook will be released with OX Sports.
