Overview

Our class had the great honor of helping the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum with redesigning their iOS, and Android app. It has been an extremely rewarding experience, re-learning what the people of the Holocaust had experienced has been such a touching and emotionally charged endeavor but also really helps push me into doing the best I can in every step of the design process.

We have 8 students in our cohort and we were split into two groups iOS and Android to help in the development of two native apps for the museum, I was placed into the Android group, being that I am the only one in the group who has a history with Material design I was quickly put in more of a leadership role by maintaining the dignity of the Android app.

Our goal is to create a similar app experience to both systems but also to create a distinctive iOS and Android app. While keeping in mind the museum's goals to Empathise, Engage, Act, & Inspire.

Before we started we had a brief about what the museum would like the app to be and who would they like it to be for.

Research

Our first step for our project was to go over what information the museum would like to accomplish. To do this we created groups of assumptions, app goals, what we do know, what we don't know, app and business goals, then we filled them with things we thought of and ideated on to be able to start researching.

As a piece of research, we created a survey for our targeted audience to understand what they think about current events. Also instead of asking a good amount about the Holocaust, we wanted to correlate it to their lives so we asked questions about bullying (if they have been, or witnessed others being bullied) It was extremely helpful in the creation of our personas.

As a team, we discovered 3 distinct personas we would like to target with our app.

From all of this information, we were able to create a vision of what our app should accomplish and was able to then create our brief.

From our User Story Map we went on to create our User flows, I was tasked with creating our "Get Involved" section.

When we all came together the day after creating this we decided that there were too many screens to create and we saw ourselves deal with the very real fear of scope creep. We decided to stop and talk about what we need and what we don't and what can be condensed to create a more manageable app and a more enjoyable experience.

So because of this, we were able to condense the information into 5 sections: Holocaust Content, Current, Getting Involved, Profile, and Booked Content. With this information, we were able to create our Site Map.

With all of this, we moved onto Sketching.

Sketching & Wireframes

We eventually narrowed all of these ideas into a few categories: Holocaust, Current, Impact, Resources, and Profile. We realized that the booked content became really overwhelming and something that we weren't sure the users would want, we still kept this concept but moved it into the profile section. We also helped break up some Holocaust material and Museum info by instead of having the bookmarked section we would have a section called Resources.

Conclusions

This project was an extremely emotionally charged project. The Holocaust was a terrible situation in history, one I had a painful but meaningful look back on during the process of this project.

I had the most amazing pleasure to help the museum meet its goals. So that they can help educate and inspire the new generation about this time in history and situations here and now that are still experiencing this kind of cruel torture. I truly hope this app will help this generation and next generation find ways to help others and create that empathy for others, helping the world become a better place to live in.
