Problems Discovered

I will pair the problems with my solutions

So one of the issues users discovered were Log In Issues. It was not intuitive for them initially, and while I understand in most cases when using a site like VRBO logging on and signing up isn’t exactly the first thing you would do, but since the tasks included in the project was for them to log in or sign up it was confusing for them to find the issues. While researching this issue I came across Expedia.com, they simplified the search terms, while also having a “Log In” option on the front as well. Combining research and my own design.
One of the main issues Users had was to do with the Search Results. It was so difficult for them to find out their search itinerary and it was hard for them to change any options within the search options instead of going all the way back to the beginning to change the options. It was also hard for the users to Sort the selections due to them not having the intuitive mapping that is needed for such things and finally Users didn’t like how the filter and the search were two different pages, it took them out of an immersive experience and also took away any signifiers that anything from the search results had changed.

The overwhelming amount of issues came when Users would use the filter option. It came up over, and over again. They couldn’t see which Filters were active, the Terms felt foreign to them, and the overall UI had usability issues for the user.

The research for this I wanted to see how other sites created a solution for this issue, and the best one I found was from Hotels.com They created a wonderful way to have the filter and the search results fit on the screen without having two separate options.

The last minor issue was found to just be a slight oversight of the user’s everyday expectations. An overwhelming amount of sites now and days create a profile picture as the way to go to their profile. Unfortunately, VRBO’s User picture is not clickable creating a slight frustration for the user.
